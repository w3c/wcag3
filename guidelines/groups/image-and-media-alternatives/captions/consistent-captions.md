:term[Captions] are presented consistently throughout the media, and across related productions, unless exceptions are :term[essential]. This includes consistent styling and placement of the captions text and consistent :term[methods] for identifying speakers, languages, and sounds.

:::ednote
* Need to define "consistently"
* Need to clarify "related productions"
* User Needs:
  * As a person who is deaf or hard of hearing, 
      * I need the style and format of captions to be consistent.
      * I need to have the speaker identified in the captions when there is more than one speaker.
      * I need to know when there is a change in the language being spoken in the audio.
      * I need to have all important sound effects and significant audio identified or described in the captions.
* Method: Present captions consistently throughout the audio content.
* Techniques:
  * (ALL) Determine how to consistently style the captions throughout the media.
  * (ALL) Determine how to consistently identify
      * the speaker in the captions.
      * the language of the speaker in the captions.
      * significant sound effects and non-spoken audio in the captions.
  * (ALL) Play the media with captions on and determine
      * if the captions are consistently styled throughout the media.
      * if the speaker is consistently identified in the captions when there is more than one speaker present.
      * if the language of the speaker is consistently identified in the captions when there is more than one language present.
      * if all significant sound effects and non-spoken audio are consistently identified in the captions when they occur.
:::

:::tests
<b>Procedure</b>
For each media asset with audio content:
1. Play the media with captions on.
2. Check that the captions are presented consistently throughout the audio content.

<b>Expected Results</b>
* #2 is true.
:::
