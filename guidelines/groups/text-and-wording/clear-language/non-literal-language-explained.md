title: Non-literal language explained
---

Explanations or unambiguous alternatives are available in :term[text content] for :term[non-literal language], such as idioms and metaphors.

:::except-when
<p>text content is:</p>

* poetic,
* scriptural,
* artistic, or
* expressive rather than informational. 
:::

:::ednote
Translation software and other tools can aid :term[content authors] in identifying non-literal language.
:::

:::tests

<b>Procedure</b>

For each phrase of :term[non-literal language] in :term[text content]: 
1. Check that access is provided to an unambiguous alternative or to an explanation of the non-literal text.
2. Check that the non-literal text is presented in a way that is available to :term[user agents], including :term[assistive technologies] (AT).
3. Check that the :term[accessibility support set] meets ‘Non-literal language explained’.

<b>Expected results</b>
* #1 and #2 are true, or 
* #3 is true.
:::
