## User needs

* As a person with color vision deficiency, I need to be able to distinguish between colored lines on a chart or map.
* As a person with low vision, I need to be able to distinguish between two states of an element without relying on a change in color alone.
* As a person with low vision, I need content, such as text and vector graphics, to be scalable and have sufficient contrast.
* As a person with dementia, age-related forgetfulness, or reduced processing speed, I need text and interactive elements to have sufficient contrast so that I can read and identify them easily.
* As a person with dementia, age-related forgetfulness, or reduced processing speed, I need non-distracting backgrounds so that I can focus on the content.
* As a person with low vision, I need to be able to distinguish important information within data-graphics such as charts, maps, and graphs.
* As a person who is deaf or hard of hearing and uses extended reality, I need a visual indicator or signal, in addition to audio, to direct me toward the source of a sound or to indicate who is speaking.
* As a person with color vision deficiency, I need information conveyed through color to also be available through other visual or audio cues.
* As a person who is deaf, I need important audio notifications and other information conveyed through sound to be available in an alternative format.
* As a person who is blind or has low vision, I need access to the visual information presented in synchronized media.
* As a person who is deaf, hard of hearing, or blind, or who has low vision, or a cognitive or learning disability, I need content to be available in multiple ways and formats.
