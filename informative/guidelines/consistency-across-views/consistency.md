## User needs

* As a person with a cognitive or learning disability who finds inconsistent  interactions confusing, I need the operations of user interface components to be consistent within a website or application.
* As a person with a cognitive or learning disability, I want to be able to easily cancel a subscription from a predictable location.
* As a person with a cognitive or learning disability, I need content and interfaces to use consistent layouts.
* As a person who is blind or has low vision, or with a motor, cognitive or learning disability, I need one or more easy ways to find content throughout a website or application.
* As a person with a cognitive or learning disability, I need what I learn on one screen about the purpose of controls and navigation in a website or application to apply to other screens in the same section of the website or application.
* As a person with low vision or a cognitive or learning disability, I need non-interactive elements, such as notification banners and typography, to be presented consistently throughout a website or application.
* As a person with a cognitive or learning disability, I need content to use a consistent tone of voice and clear and simple language at a reading level that’s not more advanced than [lower secondary education level](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG22/Understanding/reading-level.html#dfn-lower-secondary-education-level).
* As a person who is blind, or who has low vision, or a motor, cognitive or learning disability, I need the organization of  content to remain consistent so that the mental model I have built continues to be useful.
* As a person who is blind, or who has low vision, or a motor, cognitive or learning disability, I need the order of content and interactions to remain consistent throughout my session so that I can rely on my understanding of how to interact with the content.
